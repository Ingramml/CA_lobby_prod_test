About California Lobbying Transparency

The California Political Reform Act of 1974 requires the registration and disclosure of lobbying activities to promote transparency in government. The Secretary of State maintains this comprehensive database to provide public access to information about registered lobbying organizations, their expenditures, and activities.

This database contains information filed by lobbyists, lobbying firms, and lobbyist employers in accordance with California Government Code sections 86100-86300. The information is updated regularly as new filings are received and processed.

Public access to lobbying information is essential for government accountability and informed civic participation. This system is designed to make California's lobbying data easily searchable and accessible to all citizens, journalists, researchers, and government officials.

Data Disclaimer

The information contained in this database is compiled from documents filed by lobbyists, lobbying firms, and lobbyist employers. The Secretary of State makes no warranties, expressed or implied, concerning the accuracy, completeness, reliability, or suitability of this data. This information is provided as a public service and should be independently verified before being used for official purposes. Data is updated regularly but may not reflect the most recent filings. For questions about specific filings or to report discrepancies, please contact the Lobbying Division.
